Eagle Scout Project: Masks for the Community

Completed: February 28, 2021 Location: Mt. Juliet, TN

For my Eagle Scout project, I led a team of volunteers to produce and distribute over 500 handmade face masks to local schools and community members during the COVID-19 pandemic. This project involved coordinating volunteers, managing materials, and ensuring quality control to provide essential protective equipment to those in need.

Summit Award Project: Camp Discovery Dumpster Enclosure

Completed: April 2025 Location: Gainesboro, TN

For my Venturing Summit Award service project, I planned and led the replacement of a deteriorated dumpster enclosure at Camp Discovery in Gainesboro, Tennessee. Camp Discovery serves individuals with special needs through summer camps and year-round rental programs, making safe and accessible facilities essential to its mission.

The existing dumpster enclosure was structurally unsound, with rotting posts, broken panels, unstable hinges, and vegetation growing through the footings, creating safety and sanitation concerns for campers, staff, and guests. The project involved organizing the demolition of the old enclosure and coordinating the construction of a safer, more durable replacement using treated lumber, metal panels, and concrete footings.

I worked closely with the project beneficiary to confirm the scope and location, then independently handled planning tasks such as measurements, material planning, volunteer coordination, safety procedures, and work sequencing. Throughout the project, I coordinated volunteers of varying ages and experience levels, assigned roles based on skill and safety considerations, and ensured compliance with Scouting safety standards.

This project provided a long-term benefit to Camp Discovery by improving safety, sanitation, and overall functionality in a high-use area of the property. On a personal level, it strengthened my leadership, organization, and communication skills, reinforcing my goal of becoming a service-minded educator and leader.
